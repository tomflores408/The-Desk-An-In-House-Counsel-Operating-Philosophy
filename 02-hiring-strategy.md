# The Hiring Strategy

Early in my career, a sales manager told me about the "turn over" - when a salesperson will pass a difficult customer to a salesperson with a different personality. The strategy is to have people on your team that have a wide varity of personalities.  

- Big-picture thinker? Hire detail-obsessed operators.
- Impatient? Hire people with endless patience.
- Disorganized? Hire people who loved process and systems.
- Thrive on conflict? Hire diplomats who could talk people off ledges.

The result was immediate. The quality of our work improved. Clients felt the difference. Morale improved. And deal velocity increased.

The lesson is this: Your job is not to build a clone army. It is to build a team that works because the members are fundamentally different from you in ways that matter. Hire for the gaps.
