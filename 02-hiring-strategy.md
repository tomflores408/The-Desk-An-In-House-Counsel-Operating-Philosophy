# The Hiring Strategy

Early in my career, I made the classic founder mistake: I hired people who reminded me of myself.

The office was full of people with my exact strengths—and my exact weaknesses. We had great ideas but struggled with execution. We were loud where we should have been quiet, and disorganized where we needed structure.

To scale, I had to check my ego.

I started hiring people whose strengths were my weaknesses. I looked for people who loved the tasks I dreaded.

- I'm a big-picture thinker? I hired detail-obsessed operators.
- I'm impatient? I hired people with endless patience.
- I'm disorganized? I hired people who loved process and systems.
- I thrive on conflict? I hired diplomats who could talk people off ledges.

The result was immediate. The quality of our work improved. Clients felt the difference. Morale improved. And deal velocity increased.

The lesson is this: Your job is not to build a clone army. It is to build a team that works because the members are fundamentally different from you in ways that matter. Hire for the gaps.
