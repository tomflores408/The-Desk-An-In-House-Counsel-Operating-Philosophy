# Removing Friction

One of the simplest but most powerful insights is this: salespeople are at their best when they are allowed to sell.

When I examined our top performers, I realized they were spending only 40% of their week actually selling. The other 60% was consumed by administration, internal approvals, desk work, and coordination.

We began a methodical elimination campaign:

- Contract negotiation? Handled by the legal team.
- Technical questions? Routed directly to engineers.
- Pricing structures? Pre-approved and documented.
- Travel and logistics? Handled by operations.
- Follow-up paperwork? Handled by support staff.

The result was immediate. When salespeople were allowed to spend 100% of their energy selling, they didn't just close more deals—they were happier doing it.

Sales and Marketing arts require peace. When people are scared, upset, sad, or fatigued, they cannot sell or create. Indeed, the Italian Renaissance was only possible during the years of peace.

It is the duty of the Legal Team to ensure that creatives are protected from distraction. Attorneys are trained in handling disputes and administration. Problems that would give other people ulcers do not even register with us.

Look at your top performers. Calculate how many hours a week they spend on tasks that don't drive revenue. Eliminate those tasks, and you unlock growth without hiring a single new salesperson.
